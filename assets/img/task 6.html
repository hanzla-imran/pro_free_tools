When it comes towebsite builders, they say there’s beauty in simplicity – but
how simple is too simple? That’s what we wanted to find out when we tried
and tested One.com, the website builder formerly known as SimpleSite.

One.comdoes exactly what it says on the tin. It’s a simple, basic website
builder that offers an easy way for non-coders to get online.

We’ve got to be honest, though – One.com isn’t right for everybody. It’s best
for total beginners who want a very basic site without much worry over the
quality of their designs. If you need a sleek or powerful website, One.com
won’t tick your boxes.

What is One.com?


One.com began life in 2003 as SimpleSite. The website builder, founded by
brothers Jacob and Morton Elk, enables anyone – even those without coding
skills – to create and design an online presence for their personal or
professional brand.

As of 2022, SimpleSite had around 50,000 customers. Then, in that same year,
it was acquired by Group.ONE, the parent company of One.com. SimpleSite –
as it was then called – began migrating customers to One.com’s platform in
late September 2022, and SimpleSite customers are now One.com customers.

How Easy is One.com To Use?


Ease of use is one of the main things to think about when you’re choosing a
website builder. It’s important that your builder matches your own technical
skills – that way, you can actually enjoy building your website!

Unsurprisingly, this is where One.com really shines. It lives up to its name,
giving its users a straightforward and easy way to build basic websites.
One.com values pleasure over perfection, which shows in its simple editor.

One.com makes it easy for beginners to build a basic website, without any coding or tech
knowledge.

Just a heads up, though – if you want lots of creative freedom, then you’ll find
One.com frustrating rather than easy. It’s not terribly intuitive, either,
meaning straying from the set path can cause headaches.

If you want more freedom, builders like Wix or Squarespace are much better
suited for your needs. However, for the technically challenged, One.com’s
clear, unfussy editor is a godsend.

When you start out, One.com will ask you a few basic questions about your
business – such as its name, and the industry you operate in – as well as
request that you upload some of your brand assets, like logos or other
relevant images. If you don’t feel like doing this right away, you can skip this
part, and return to it later.

To start, One.com will ask you a few questions about your business, as well as requesting that you
add a few resources – such as your brand's logo – for a more personalized, tailored outcome.

One.comthen guides you through the building process – you simply choose
your color scheme, add images, a title, and domain name, then create a login.
This final step automatically publishes your site, which you can then go back
and finish editing.

It’s super simple, and totally beginner friendly. That said, it wasn’t always the
most intuitive – at times, its simplicity became extremely restrictive, and
made it difficult to work out how to do pretty basic things. In one round of
testing, it took us a long time just to work out how to edit text before
publishing our site, which should be easy!

One.com claims you can build a site within a matter of minutes. In our testing,
we found that in reality, this is likely to turn into two hours (assuming you
want a well-rounded website).

One.com Theme Design and Customization


One.commight be simple to build, but how will your website look? The truth
is, design is the price you pay for the straightforward editor. One.com’s
designs are pretty dated, and offer very limited customization.

That said, some of its templates – while not wholly inspiring, perhaps – are
extremely functional, and there are different options to pick from depending
on which industry you’re in.

One.com enables you to choose from a number of themes to get your website started.

Once you’ve selected a template, you’ll be able to customize it further with
colors and images relevant to your brand.

Once you've selected your theme, you'll be prompted to customize it with the colors and design of
your brand, ensuring you nail your business's distinct look and feel.

Then, it’s time to get stuck in and start editing your One.com website’s text.

The bad news? Customization is about as basic as it comes. The good news,
though, is that One.com makes it very easy to add elements, such as text and
images.

Next, it's time to edit your website: replacing the placeholder text with inspiring, compelling, and
relevant headlines and descriptions of your business and brand.

However, editing or readjusting them once they’re on your page is a more
challenging – and often impossible – task.

It’s easy to switch your site’s layout after publishing, but you can’t go back and
choose a totally new theme – you can just change the layout of the theme you
picked originally.

Think of One.com like Playmobil – you can easily build a good-looking site, but
it’s within the restrictions laid out for you. Wix and Squarespace, for example,
are more like Lego – still easy for beginners, but with the potential for more
freedom, imagination, and impressive results.

What are One.com's Features Like?


While design is the first thing your visitors will notice, it’s the features that can
make a long-term difference to your site, affecting how likely it is to succeed
and grow. So how does One.com stack up?

Well, good news on this front. When we reviewed SimpleSite – before it was
acquired by One.com – we bemoaned the former’s sadly limited range of
features, which combined to make it less than ideal for ambitious websites.

With One.com, however, you’re treated to a generous wealth of features and
integrations, including Google Ads, one-click WordPress installation,
automatic backups, and excellent website reporting and analytics tools.

One.com's litany of features represents a big improvement over its SimpleSite days.

Generous Ecommerce

One.com’s main triumph when it comes to features is the fact it lets you sell up
to five products on its free plan. That’s really worth shouting about.
Ecommerce capability is usually reserved for paying customers, so offering it
free of charge really makes One.com stand out from its competitors.

This is perfect if you want to sell a few items, but we don’t recommend
One.com if you’re actually going to start an ecommerce store. It’s not very
scalable, and won’t give you the tools you need to manage your store properly.

To unlock unlimited products for your online store, you have to upgrade to the
most expensiveE-Commerce planfor $30.49 per month. At this price, you
might as well use a better ecommerce platform, such as Shopify or
BigCommerce, which makes it a great choice for making money from your hobby.
It’s a rare offer, and is definitely worth making the most of!

Overall,One.com is very generous with its five product ecommerce feature on
itsfree plan,which makes it a great choice for making money from your hobby.
It’s a rare offer, and is definitely worth making the most of!

On-the-Go Mobile Optimization

Most website builders offer mobile-optimized templates, so that your site will
automatically look great on any screen. One.com is no different, but where it
really sets itself apart is with its on-the-go mobile website editor.

One.com automatically adapts your website for mobile to make it responsive on all devices – not
just desktop computers!

You can edit and manage your site straight from your mobile, without
downloading an app or fiddling around with plugins – just a few taps on your
phone or tablet, and you’re good to go!

Professional Email Addresses

When you buy a domain through One.com, you also get five personal email
addresses included. So rather than using your generic email, such as
joebloggs@gmail.com, instead you can have joebloggs@ceramicsworld.com.
This is a great feature, as it gives you and your site a more professional image.

These are the three main perks that come with using One.com. However,
there are some key features that One.com does not include, which you would
expect to see from a typical website builder.

Poor SEO

Search Engine Optimization (SEO) is essential for getting your website found
– without it, your website doesn’t stand a chance of ranking highly in Google’s
search results. Thebest SEO wesite buildersinclude tons of SEO tools
automatically, and make it as easy as possible for you to optimize your site
even further.

One.com just doesn’t step up enough when it comes to SEO.

Yes, it gives you mobile-optimized pages, which Google loves, and has guides
showing you how to use keywords in your headings. But you’re very limited in
what you can do to optimize your site. For starters, you can’t install any apps,
insert Google Analytics, or access the code of your pages.

When you compare One.com’s SEO offerings to that of Squarespace or Wix,
you realize how basic it is. If you’re aiming for the first spot, or even first page
of Google’s search results, One.com is simply not for you.

Non-Existent App Market

One.com doesn’t have an app market, meaning you’re totally reliant on its
built-in features. It’s not the only website builder to do this – Squarespace, for
example, doesn’t have an app market, but then again it also has some of the
best quality features around. One.com can’t boast the same!

Outdated Social Media Integration

While you can embed social buttons so that visitors can share your site across
platforms like Facebook or Twitter/X, you can’t insert live social feeds onto
your site. This is a real shame – social media is a huge part of modern
marketing, and your website is the perfect place to give visitors a chance to
keep up to date with your online profiles.

All in all,One.com has a lot less to offer than its competitors when it comes to
features. Sure, you’ll get the basics, with a few nice perks thrown in – but
overall, the glaring gaps in its features list are hard to ignore, or to justify.

The truth is, if you want to scale up your website, you’ll find yourself
struggling.

Is One.com's Help and Support Up to Scratch?


Help and support is easy to overlook when you’re choosing a website builder,
but it’s worth checking out before committing. Nobody wants to spend hours
going round in circles with an unhelpful chat bot – so what does One.com have
to offer in your hour of need?

The answer is – not much. One.com only offers one type of support: online.

You can either start a live chat with a One.com customer service
representative – fortunately, 24/7 – or wait a little longer for email support.

This isn’t ideal (the lack of phone support is particularly galling) but it does
represent an improvement on SimpleSite’s customer support, which only
offered email support before being acquired by One.com.

One.com makes it easy and welcoming to access customer support online.

With live chat and email available, this at least means your issue is being dealt
with by a human being. But the downside is, they can only work at a human
pace.

The average response time for One.com’s email support is two working days,
which is less than ideal if you have an urgent issue.

There’s no phone number listed on One.com’s customer support page – only
the email, postal address, and list of FAQs. You can head to the blog for more
general information, but for specific or technical problems, you’ll need to
contact the support team directly.

How Much Does One.com Cost?


One.com has four plans to choose from:the Beginner, Explorer, and
Enthusiast, and Guru plans. The Beginner plan is $6.99/month; Explorer costs
$12.99/month, while Enthusiast comes in at $17.99/month. One.com’s most
expensive plan, Guru, costs $26.99/month. These prices are all when you sign
up for annual, rather than monthly, billing.

All come with a three-month free period to get you started when you first sign
up to build your website with One.com.

One.com's pricing plans start at just $6.99/month, with a three-month free trial available across
all its plans.

TheBeginner plansupports one website, includes 50GB of SSD storage,
provides unlimited mail accounts, and offers email backup. Because of its
obvious limitations, this plan it’s ideal for hobby websites or personal projects.

TheExplorer planalso supports just one website, but offers 100GB of SSD
storage, retains all the features of the Beginner plan, and adds that all-
important one-click WordPress install feature (ideal if your website is going to
be content-heavy!). It’s designed for professional websites, whether personal
or business, and for people with a bit more money to spend.

The Enthusiast plancomes with everything from the Explorer plan, but lets
you build up to five websites – and enjoy up to 200GB of SSD storage.
Unsurprisingly, this plan is best for online stores.

Remember, all paid plans come with a three-month free trial, so you can try
them out without any risk!

The Guru plansupports up to ten websites, provides 750GB of SSD storage,
and throws unlimited mail accounts – with premium features – into the
bargain. Also included is free site protection, One.com’s free SEO tool, and
advanced WordPress tools – all completely gratis.

All One.com plans come with three months free hosting, a free month of
Google Ads credit, a free SSL certificate, web analytics, and 24/7 live chat
support. Not bad!

Unlike with SimpleSite – now dearly departed – One.com doesn’t offer a free
forever plan. However, that three months free you can enjoy (on any plan!)
more than makes up for this omission.

One.com Review: Our Final Verdict


One.com proves once and for all that you don’t need tech skills to build a
website. That said, it takes the idea of simplicity a little too far, resulting in
very basic features and often frustratingly limited editing tools.

We’ve explored One.com’s features, ease of use, design, help and support,
and pricing, and it’s certainly kept us on our toes. Easy to use, but not intuitive;
lacking in basic features, but making up for it with free ecommerce and rare
mobile editing; One.com is a bag of contradictions.

One.com’s hidden gems make it a good choice for beginners looking to start a
hobby or personal website, and even sell a few items on the side. And its
feature set is impressive, as is the three free months it offers across all its paid
offerings – not just its most affordable ones.

However, if you want more stylish designs or scalable features, you’d be better
off with a different website builder.

Wix is the best overall website builder, and is really beginner-friendly to boot.
GoDaddy is the fastest if you just want something online quickly, Squarespace
has the best designs, while Shopify is best for growing online stores.

One.comcan’t compete with these more up to date builders, but it’s not trying
to. As it says on its website, “using our product should feel enjoyable – like
playing a game” – and that’s exactly what it achieves. It’s not a serious builder
– it’s playful, so enjoy using it. Just don’t expect your Playmobil passion to roll
in the big bucks anytime soon.
